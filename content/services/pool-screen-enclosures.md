## What Makes Pool Screen Enclosures Special

Pool enclosures are distinct from general [screen enclosures](/services/screen-enclosures/) because they must meet specific Florida safety and building code requirements. These are fully enclosed structures surrounding your swimming pool, typically with one or more doors for entry and exit. The mesh excludes insects while allowing water visibility and circulation.

In Port Orange, Daytona Beach, and throughout Volusia County, pool enclosures have become standard. The combination of insect protection, debris control, and weather protection makes them essential for full pool enjoyment.

## Florida Pool Enclosure Requirements

Florida's pool barrier law (also called the pool safety fence law) mandates that swimming pools be isolated from your home and landscape using approved barriers. Pool screen enclosures satisfy this requirement by creating a complete barrier structure. An approved enclosure means you meet Florida Building Code requirements and satisfy insurance company standards.

As of June 2025, Volusia County exempted standard residential fences from requiring permits, but pool barriers—whether fencing or screens—still require permits and inspection. We handle all permitting requirements for Port Orange, Daytona Beach, and throughout Volusia County to ensure your enclosure fully complies.

Mesh standards matter significantly. ASTM F1346 specifies mesh requirements for pool enclosures—specifically, mesh opening size that prevents small children from passing through. We use ASTM F1346-compliant mesh exclusively.

## Pool Enclosure Designs

**Full Enclosures** completely surround your pool with screening and solid frame structure. Doors allow entry and exit. Full enclosures are most common in Port Orange and Daytona Beach.

**Lanai Enclosures** enclose decking areas adjacent to pools, creating furnished outdoor rooms that can include dining areas, lounging spaces, and outdoor kitchens. Popular in upscale Daytona Beach neighborhoods.

**Three-Season Enclosures** combine screening with solid panels for partial weather and climate protection. Popular in northern Volusia County where cooler months occasionally occur.

**Hybrid Screens with Lower Panels** have solid lower panels (privacy, debris protection) and screen upper panels (light, visibility). Especially popular for pool-side enclosures in family homes.

**Motorized Screen Systems** allow you to open and close screens with remote controls or automated systems. Perfect for pool areas where flexibility and convenience matter. Screens deploy for entertaining, retract for cleaning, or close automatically before storms.

## Materials for Pool Environments

**Marine-Grade Aluminum Frames** are essential for pool enclosures. Pools create a wet, corrosive environment with chlorine exposure. We use aluminum specifically engineered for pool environments in coastal Daytona Beach and throughout Volusia County.

**Stainless Steel Hardware**: Fasteners, hinges, latches, and locks must be stainless steel to resist chlorine corrosion. Standard fasteners rust rapidly in pool environments.

**ASTM F1346-Compliant Mesh**: This specialized mesh meets Florida's pool safety standards. We exclusively use compliant mesh, ensuring your enclosure meets all code requirements.

**Reinforced Structure**: Pool enclosures experience greater wind loads than standard screens because they're often larger structures. We build reinforced frames designed for hurricane-force winds across all of Volusia County.

## Maintenance and Durability

Pool enclosures require regular cleaning due to chlorine exposure. Monthly washing with mild soap and fresh water extends material life. Annually, inspect all fasteners and hardware for corrosion and tighten as needed. Check doors for proper function and alignment.

A properly maintained pool enclosure lasts 15–20+ years in Volusia County. Mesh eventually requires replacement ([rescreening](/services/rescreening/)) after 7–10 years. The structural frame lasts decades when properly maintained.
