## Design Considerations for Volusia County Properties

Your patio upgrade should reflect your lifestyle and property's characteristics. High-use entertaining spaces benefit from comprehensive coverage—screens plus shade plus perhaps roof panels. Quiet relaxation spaces might emphasize shade with minimal enclosure. Swimming pool patios need different solutions than vegetable gardens.

We assess your patio's orientation, sun exposure pattern, prevailing wind direction, and vegetation. South and west-facing patios need aggressive shade. North-facing spaces might prioritize screening and windbreak function. Patios with ocean breezes need wind-resistant designs. Properties with surrounding vegetation have different pest challenges than open areas.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Should I screen my patio or add shade, or both?
Both provide different benefits. Screens protect from insects but don't reduce heat. Shade reduces heat but doesn't protect from bugs. Many Port Orange and Daytona Beach homeowners combine both—screens for bug protection and partial shade structures for heat reduction. The combination creates year-round usable outdoor rooms.

### Q: Will an upgraded patio really increase my home's value?
Yes, measurably. A quality screened patio adds 4–8% to your home's value. A covered patio with shade adds 3–6%. Combined systems add even more. Beyond financial value, you get immediate daily benefit—an upgraded patio becomes your favorite room in your home.

### Q: Can you upgrade an existing patio, or do I need new construction?
We upgrade existing patios by adding screens, shade structures, and covers to your existing slab or deck. This is much more affordable than new construction and delivers the same benefit. Almost any existing patio can be upgraded.
