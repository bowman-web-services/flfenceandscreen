## Why Screen Repair Matters in Florida

Living in Daytona Beach, Port Orange, Ormond Beach, or New Smyrna Beach means dealing with more than just everyday wear and tear. Tropical storms and hurricane-force winds can damage screens in seconds, and Florida's intense UV rays break down mesh material faster than in other climates. Salt air corrosion near coastal areas like Ponce Inlet and Edgewater accelerates deterioration, while the combination of heat and humidity weakens mesh fibers and frame joints over time. What starts as a small hole becomes a major problem when you're fighting bugs during peak season.

## Our Screen Repair Solutions

We handle every type of screen damage with professional precision. Torn mesh is the most common issue—whether it's a clean tear or multiple punctures, we replace just the mesh panel rather than the entire screen when possible, saving you money. Damaged frames from impact or settling can be straightened or reinforced. Bent and broken hardware—springs, hinges, and corner brackets—we replace with commercial-grade components. Damaged screen doors get special attention since they take constant daily abuse in Florida homes.

Our team assesses each repair carefully. Sometimes repairing a single panel makes perfect sense. Other times, when a screen frame is severely compromised or shows widespread corrosion, [rescreening the entire frame](/services/rescreening/) becomes the smarter investment. We'll walk you through the options and never oversell you on work you don't need.

We use only high-quality mesh materials designed for Florida's climate—18-by-16 mesh for standard protection, and for customers who need extra protection against no-see-ums and love bugs, we offer 20-by-20 mesh that still allows excellent visibility and airflow. Our hardware is stainless steel or powder-coated aluminum to resist saltwater corrosion, crucial for homes anywhere in Volusia and Flagler Counties.

## Materials and Options

Standard fiberglass mesh is our go-to for most repairs because it's affordable, durable, and offers excellent visibility. For coastal properties in Daytona Beach, New Smyrna Beach, and other beach communities, we recommend marine-grade stainless steel mesh that won't corrode from salt air. We also install pet-resistant mesh if you have dogs or cats that tend to lean against screens—it's thicker and far more durable than standard fiberglass.

Specialty mesh options include solar screens that reduce heat gain (important in Florida summers), and security mesh for added peace of mind. Whatever your needs, we source materials built to withstand Florida's demanding climate.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can you repair a screen without replacing the whole frame?
Yes, in most cases. We can replace just the mesh panel if the frame is still solid, or repair specific frame sections if they're damaged. We assess each screen and recommend the most cost-effective solution.

### Q: How long does a screen repair typically take?
Simple repairs—a small mesh patch or hardware adjustment—usually take under an hour. Full mesh replacement in a frame takes 2–3 hours. We aim to complete most repairs same-day when possible.

### Q: Will my repaired screen be as good as the original?
Absolutely. We use high-quality materials that often outlast the original, especially if you upgrade to a more durable mesh variety. A properly repaired screen functions exactly as it did when new.
