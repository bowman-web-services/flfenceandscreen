## Why Aluminum Rules in Florida

Aluminum dominates outdoor structure construction in Volusia County because it solves problems other materials create:

**Corrosion Resistance**: Unlike steel, aluminum doesn't rust. Powder-coated finishes resist salt-air corrosion crucial in coastal Daytona Beach, Ponce Inlet, and Edgewater. In humid Port Orange and Deltona, aluminum maintains integrity year after year without degradation.

**Lightweight Strength**: Aluminum is strong yet lightweight. It's easy to work with during installation and allows larger spans without excessive support members. You get open, unobstructed outdoor spaces rather than heavily-braced structures.

**Maintenance-Minimal**: Aluminum requires virtually no maintenance. No painting, no sealing, no rust treatment. Annual inspection for loose hardware is sufficient. This matters tremendously for busy homeowners throughout Volusia County.

**Design Flexibility**: Aluminum can be shaped, fabricated, and finished in countless ways. Contemporary minimalist designs, traditional detailed structures, custom curves—aluminum accommodates any aesthetic preference.

**Durability**: Quality aluminum structures last 30–50+ years with minimal maintenance. This longevity represents exceptional value compared to alternatives.

## Aluminum Materials and Specifications

**Aluminum Extrusions**: Custom-shaped aluminum beams, channels, and profiles engineered for structural requirements. Available in standard and custom profiles. Larger projects often use standard extrusions; custom designs utilize specialized profiles.

**Aluminum Sheet and Plate**: Flat aluminum materials used for panels, trim, and specialized applications. Available in various alloys and thicknesses matched to structural requirements.

**Powder-Coated Finishes**: Factory-applied coating providing durability and aesthetic appeal. Available in dozens of colors matching home aesthetics. Coastal properties benefit from premium coastal-grade coatings.

**Stainless Steel Hardware**: All fasteners, hinges, and latches are stainless steel, essential for Volusia County's coastal and humid environment. Standard fasteners corrode rapidly; stainless steel maintains integrity.

**Structural Engineering**: Our aluminum designs meet or exceed Florida Building Code requirements for wind load. All structures are engineered for hurricane-force conditions throughout Volusia County.
